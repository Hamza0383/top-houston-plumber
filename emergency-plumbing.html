What Counts as a Plumbing Emergency?

If you're unsure whether your situation requires immediate help, here are the most common emergency scenarios we respond to in Houston every day.

💥

Burst or Ruptured Pipe

A pipe that has burst is releasing water continuously. Every second counts — shut off the water and call immediately.

🚽

Sewage Backup

Raw sewage entering your home is a health emergency. Multiple drains backing up at once signal a main sewer blockage.

🌊

Active Flooding

Whether from a failed appliance connection or a broken supply line, standing water inside your home needs immediate attention.

🔥

Water Heater Failure

A leaking or over-pressurized water heater poses safety risks. Pooling water around the unit means you need help right away.

⚠️

Gas Line Plumbing Issue

If you smell gas near water heater connections or gas-fed appliances, leave the building and call emergency services, then call us.

🔩

Complete Loss of Water

Sudden total loss of water pressure in your Houston home may indicate a main line break that needs urgent assessment.

Emergency Plumbing FAQ

What should I do while waiting for an emergency plumber? +
Shut off the main water supply to stop active water flow. For sewage backups, avoid using any drains or toilets. Move valuables away from flooding water. Open windows if you suspect a gas issue and wait outside. Our team will give you specific guidance when you call.
Do you really respond at night and on weekends? +
Yes — genuinely. We staff emergency plumbers around the clock, including weekends and holidays. Houston plumbing emergencies don't follow a 9-to-5 schedule, and our response doesn't either. When you call (713) 379-8568, a real person answers regardless of the hour.
How quickly can a plumber reach my Houston home? +
Response times vary based on your location and current call volume, but we work to dispatch the nearest available plumber as quickly as possible. For active flooding or burst pipes, we treat those calls with the highest urgency.
My water heater is leaking — is that an emergency? +
Yes, a leaking water heater should be treated urgently. It can cause floor damage, mold, and in the case of a faulty pressure relief valve, a serious safety hazard. Turn off the water supply to the unit and call us immediately.

Warning Signs Your Water Heater Needs Attention Now

Don't wait until you're dealing with a complete failure or water damage. These signs mean it's time to call a Houston plumber.

🥶

No Hot Water at All

Complete absence of hot water points to a failed heating element, tripped breaker, pilot light issue, or a failed unit that needs replacement.

🌡️

Inconsistent Water Temperature

Water that fluctuates between hot and cold or never reaches the right temperature signals a failing heating element or thermostat problem.

🟤

Rusty or Discolored Hot Water

Brown or orange water from your hot taps means corrosion inside the tank. This is a sign the anode rod has failed and the tank is rusting from within.

💥

Popping or Rumbling Sounds

Sediment buildup on the tank floor hardens and makes loud noises as the unit heats. Left unaddressed, this causes overheating and accelerates tank failure.

💧

Water Pooling Around the Unit

A leaking water heater is an urgent problem. Even a small puddle can indicate a cracked tank or failing pressure relief valve — both need immediate attention.

📅

Unit Older Than 10 Years

Most tank water heaters have a lifespan of 8–12 years. If yours is approaching or past that, a failure isn't a matter of "if" — it's "when."

Water Heater Questions — Answered

How long do water heaters last in Houston? +
Most traditional tank water heaters last 8–12 years with proper maintenance. Houston's hard water can shorten that lifespan by accelerating sediment buildup and corrosion. Tankless units typically last 15–20 years. If your unit is approaching the end of its expected lifespan, it's worth having it inspected proactively rather than waiting for a complete failure.
My hot water runs out quickly — what does that mean? +
Running out of hot water quickly usually means one of your heating elements has failed (common in electric units), or sediment has built up so extensively it has reduced the effective capacity of the tank. In some cases it simply means your household has outgrown the tank size. Our plumbers can diagnose the exact cause quickly.
Is a leaking water heater dangerous? +
Yes — and it needs immediate attention. A small drip from a fitting or connection can sometimes be repaired, but water leaking from the tank body means the tank has corroded through and needs replacement. A faulty pressure relief valve is a safety hazard that should be addressed the same day. If you see water pooling around your water heater, call us now.
Can you install a new water heater the same day? +
In most cases, yes. We carry common tank sizes and standard components on our service vehicles. If you need a specific size or a tankless unit not in our standard inventory, we can typically source and install it the same or next business day.
